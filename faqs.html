Answers to common questions regarding CCTV systems

1. Answers to common questions regarding CCTV systems

Why do I need a surveillance grade hard disk drive (HDD) instead of a generic device? There are three main tasks HDDs do besides storage: read data, write data, and transfer data. Generally, surveillance HDDs are turned on 24 hours/7 days a week. They write data and store footage from security video 90% of the time. They transfer them only 10% of the time. Regular HDDs are designed for being used mostly for reading and when used in a surveillance environment, the continuous demand of writing videos will cause the hardware to eventually fail. Video surveillance HDDs are designed to endure heavy writing, from several closed-circuit television (CCTV) cameras simultaneously, consume low power, and endure weather fluctuations unlike regular HDDs.
