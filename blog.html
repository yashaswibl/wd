How CCTV Surveillance Systems can aid in the battle against Coronavirus

As COVID-19 ravages the globe, previously seldom heard words like quarantine and pandemic have become commonplace. Besides lives, livelihoods are also at stake and most countries have lifted the lockdown or are in the process of lifting it. However much we pine to go back to normal as it was before the outbreak, we know that we can only have a 'new normal'. The post-COVID world is one where a vaccine or cure is still on the wish-list, the virus is at large and safety is a more pressing concern than ever. So far, technology has aided mankind tremendously in this battle and will continue to do so. With the lockdown lifted, the onus is on each of us to maintain social distancing and take all precautions to keep ourselves and those we care for safe. CCTV systems can be of great help in supplying the extraordinary vigilance that this situation calls for.

People are worried to go out and come into contact with a contaminant. Parents are worried that their children may take masks off when playing outside unattended, or come into contact with someone who is not masked. Managers in apartments, offices and commercial spaces with high number of visitors are living a nightmare – all it takes is one infected person or a single oversight to compromise everyone else. While the fear is understandable, what we need is care and preparedness. CCTV systems with adequate monitoring can make sure we can keep a caring, watchful eye on all risk-prone spots.

AI capabilities can be clubbed with the CCTV security system to track distance between people based on 3D geometric space-sensing and send alerts when a breach is made or likely. In public spaces like malls and shops, an automated voice alert can nudge visitors who are getting too close. Face-recognition can aid in contact tracing and prevent large numbers of potential contaminations. Heat-sensors within cameras can check if someone is feverish and alert authorities to take adequate measures. This feature is highly useful for companies who are resuming work from office, and helps all employees feel assured.

Those living in apartments and gated-communities, would feel much more in control with access to surveillance footage of common areas on their personal smartphones. Retailers can encourage customers to resume shopping by ensuring robust social-distancing and hygiene protocols via surveillance systems. India was already aspiring to build more smart cities like Hyderabad where community CCTVs have brought about major reduction in crime and increased sense of safety in citizens, especially women. Across the country, several officials are already utilizing surveillance systems from “COVID-19 War Rooms” to ensure and monitor upkeep of safety protocol. Surveillance along with technologies like geo-mapping, facial recognition and AI based alerts and recommendation will be the new norm for safety to reclaim our collective spaces in the post-COVID world.
